## Overview

Video editing is still one of the biggest bottlenecks in content creation. While AI has made editing faster, most tools either automate too little or take away too much control.

InstaCut explores a different approach—letting creators edit videos simply by describing what they want in natural language. Instead of navigating complex timelines and menus, users interact with an AI assistant that understands editing intent and performs the work automatically.

---

## The Problem

Modern video editors are powerful but overwhelming, especially for solo creators who publish content regularly.

Through research, I identified four recurring frustrations:

* Time spent removing pauses, filler words, and repetitive mistakes
* Complex interfaces that require technical editing knowledge
* Repeating the same editing style across every video
* Exporting the same content for multiple social platforms

The editing process often becomes repetitive work rather than creative work.

---

## The Opportunity

**How might we reduce editing time while keeping creators in control of the final output?**

Instead of replacing the editor, could AI become an intelligent editing partner?

---

## Research

I explored existing editing workflows, AI video tools, and creator communities to understand how people currently edit content.

Three key insights emerged.

### 01 — Editing is repetitive

Most editing time isn't spent making creative decisions—it's spent performing repetitive actions like trimming silences, adding captions, creating jump cuts, and resizing videos.

### 02 — Interfaces create friction

Traditional editing software exposes every professional control upfront, making the learning curve intimidating for beginners and unnecessary for everyday creators.

### 03 — Every creator has a unique style

Creators develop consistent pacing, transitions, zoom levels, and caption styles. Existing AI tools generate generic edits instead of adapting to personal preferences.

---

## Design Goals

The product needed to:

* Make AI editing approachable for beginners
* Keep users in control of every edit
* Reduce repetitive manual work
* Learn each creator's editing style over time
* Support multiple publishing formats from a single workflow

---

## Solution

InstaCut replaces complicated editing workflows with conversation.

Users upload a video and simply describe the result they want.

Examples include:

> "Remove all pauses and add captions."

> "Turn this into a 30-second Instagram Reel."

> "Use my usual editing style and export for YouTube Shorts."

The AI interprets the request, performs the edits, and presents a preview that users can refine before exporting.

## Designing the Experience

### Three-panel workspace

The interface balances AI interaction with familiar editing concepts.

**Media Panel**

Manage uploaded videos, assets, and project files.

**Preview Window**

Review edits in real time without leaving the conversation.

**AI Workspace**

Type editing instructions, review suggestions, and iterate naturally through conversation.

---

## Design Decisions

### Conversation over complexity

Instead of forcing users to learn editing terminology, the interface understands everyday language.

---

### AI with human control

Automation speeds up editing, but creators always review, refine, and approve the final result.

---

### Minimal visual hierarchy

A distraction-free dark interface keeps the user's attention on the content rather than the software.

---

## Challenges

One of the biggest design challenges was balancing automation with trust.

Users needed confidence that AI wouldn't make irreversible changes. This led to a workflow centered around previews, editable results, and conversational iteration rather than one-click automation.

---

## What I Learned

Designing an AI-first product taught me that automation alone isn't enough.

Great AI experiences explain their actions, preserve user control, and reduce complexity without hiding important functionality.

I also learned how interaction design changes when the primary interface becomes conversation instead of traditional UI components.

---

## Reflection

InstaCut reimagines video editing as a collaborative experience between creators and AI.

Rather than replacing creativity, the product removes repetitive work so creators can spend more time telling better stories.
